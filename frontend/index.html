Important Things to Know Before You Start!

Resolutions

Adopted resolutions are not binding to any country. They are just a way to express a concern or support a cause by a nation.

Voting Mechanism

One vote per member. Country can vote with "Yes", "No", or "Abstain" from voting.

Topics

Topics are brought to the table by UNGA members, other UN entities, and the secretary-general.

Meetings

UNGA members meet every year in September at the UN Headquarters in New York

Contraversial Issues from 1946-2018

  • Israeli-Palestinian Conflict
  • Neclear Weapons
  • Human Rights
  • Arms Control
  • Economic Development
  • Colonoliasm

Started in the late 1940s, the Israeli-Palestinian conflict is considered to be one of the most debatable topics at the UNGA voting data. The dataset shows that it accounts for around 25% of UNGA votes. Examples for the UNGA resolutions related to this ongoing conflict include the call to stop the building of new Israeli settlements on the Palestinian land, cessation of the Isreali occupation, and several demands calling for peace between the two parties.

Since its establishment, the UN endeavoured for a world free of nuclear weapons due to its inherent danger on the whole globe. The disarmament of nuclear weapons and the ban of using it for military purposes are two examples of the resolutions under this category.

Officially announced on 10 December 1948, The Universal Declaration of Human Rights (UDHR) sets the foundations for all the practices that human beings all over the world should be treated with. Article 2 of UDHR emphasises the appliance of the declared rules on all people regardless of their race, gender, religion, political opinion and any other types of discrimination. The UNGA resolutions revolve around applying the concepts declared in UDHR, or other conventions such as the Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW). Other resolutions discussed might be related to specific countries.

Similar in nature to the nuclear weapons resolutions, Arms Control resolutions focus on stopping the proliferation and development of hazardous biological and chemical weapons.

Resolutions discussing economic growth, international trade between countries, poverty eradication, and finance for development. It is also involved in advocating the rights of workers (especially women) in the workplace

Driven by the principles of "equal rights and self-determination of all people", the UNGA, successfully adopted the Declaration on Decolonization in 1960 that advocates for the rights of dependent countries. Currently, there are still 17 non-self governing territories remaining. The UNGA closely monitors these places and ensures that the administrative of that territory follows the rules and submit continuous reports on their state.
